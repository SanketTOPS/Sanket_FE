Learning HTML5: Video Tag

Introduction to HTML5 Video

The HTML5 <video> tag is used to embed video content into a webpage. It supports multiple video formats and provides a way to play, pause, and control video playback without requiring plugins like Flash.

Basic Syntax

<video controls>
    <source src="video.mp4" type="video/mp4">
    Your browser does not support the video tag.
</video>

The controls attribute adds playback controls like play, pause, and volume to the video.

Attributes of the Video Tag

  • controls: Displays video controls.
  • autoplay: Automatically plays the video when the page loads.
  • loop: Makes the video loop continuously.
  • muted: Starts the video with the audio muted.
  • poster: Specifies an image to display while the video is loading or until the user plays it.

Browser Support

The <video> tag is supported by all major modern browsers. However, not all browsers support every video format. The commonly supported formats are:

  • MP4: Using the H.264 codec.
  • WebM: Using the VP8/VP9 codec.
  • Ogg: Using the Theora codec.
